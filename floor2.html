The North Room


Exhibit Description/Subtitle

During the interior restoration of J. Pierpont Morgan’s Library in 2010, the
Room of the building was converted into a gallery designed to show a wide
selection of Morgan's collection of antiquities, featuring ancient Western
Asian seals and tablets, Greek and Roman objects acquired by Morgan,
and the Thaw Collection of early medieval objects of personal adornment.
Four Ancient Western Asian clay tablets, containing text written in cuneiform,
including the earliest known flood narrative, are also on display.The Thaw
Collection of objects created in Europe, Central Asia, and the Middle East
between 300 and 1,000 A.D. is shown in its entirety for the first time.

  1. Walk straight into The Engelhard Gallery
  2. You will find the exhibit on the backwall towards the far left side.

She Who Wrote: Enheduanna and Women of Mesopotamia


Exhibit Description/Subtitle

She Who Wrote: Enheduanna and Women of Mesopotamia, ca. 3400-2000 B.C. brings
together for the first time a comprehensive selection of artworks that
capture rich and shifting expressions of women’s lives in ancient Mesopotamia
during the 3rd millennium B.C. These works bear testament to women’s
roles in religious contexts as goddesses, priestesses, and worshippers
as well as in social, economic and political spheres as mothers, workers, and rulers.

  2. You will find the exhibit on the backwall towards the far right side.
